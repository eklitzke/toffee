welcome to toffee dns. we have a simple goal: we want to make it easier for people to host their own websites and services.

background

historically, the problem of resolving dns entries to a residential ip address has been solved by purchasing a static ip from your isp. you pay your isp a premium, and they give you an ip address that never changes. then you tell the registrar you purchased your domain name from about your ip address, and everything works.

this all works fine, but it's increasingly difficult and expensive to get a static ip address from your isp. the solution that many people today have opted into is purchasing a virtual machine on a vps like slicehost, or linode. this gives you the flexibility of having full control over a linux machine, and also gives you a public ip address and dns. this is great, but it's also expensive; typically these services cost at least $20 per year, which is much more than what you probably paid your registrar for your domain name.

the solution

what many developers really need is a simple dns service, that will always point to their home ip address, even if that address is not static. most isps hand out ips based on dhcp leases that last for a very long time, typically between twelve hours and a week. furthermore, your router knows all of the details of this dhcp lease; this means that your router knows exactly when the dhcp lease is going to expire, and when your ip address will change. if you have a smart router, it will update your dynamic dns data on demand as your ip address changes.

for a dns service with a two minute ttl (toffee's default), this means that your downtime will range from 0.28% (for a 12 hour dhcp lease) to 0.02% (for a one week dhcp lease).
